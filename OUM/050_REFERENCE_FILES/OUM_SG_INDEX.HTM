Solution Delivery Guides

  • CAS OUM (Cloud Application Services) - The CAS OUM (Cloud Application Services OUM) Solution Delivery Guide provides an overview of the approach, phases and tasks that define the Cloud Application Services OUM (CAS OUM) approach. The information in this guide enables project managers, and project team members, to better understand the full scope of Cloud Application Services implementations, and to plan and execute those engagements.

Supplemental Guides

  • Application Implementation - This guide supports the the definition and deployment of a business solution based on one or more commercial-off-the-shelf (COTS), packaged application products, or product suites, such as Oracle E-Business Suite or PeopleSoft Enterprise, etc. COTS application products are generally highly configurable, providing the flexibility needed to support a broad range of industry and regional requirements.

  • Business Intelligence (BI) - This guide supports the implementation of Business Intelligence (BI) view focuses on those OUM activities and tasks that are typically leveraged for custom Business Intelligence engagements, as well as engagements involving commercial-off-the-shelf (COTS) Business Intelligence offerings, such as Enterprise Performance Management (EPM) and other BI applications.

  • Business Process Management (BPM) Project Engineering - This guide is based on the IT Strategies from Oracle (ITSO) Business Process Engineering Practitioner Guide. The Oracle Business Process Engineering framework introduces a discipline to effectively deliver composite business application projects in a BPM environment. The framework encompasses the traditional analysis, design, implementation, and operational management and monitoring steps of software engineering, but addresses only the requirements specific to Business Process Engineering that are not covered by traditional methods and practices. In this way the Oracle Business Process Engineering framework can be used to simply augment existing practices.

  • Business Process Management (BPM) Roadmap - This guide is based on the BPM Roadmap described in the IT Strategies from Oracle (ITSO) guidelines and reference architectures library, Creating a BPM Roadmap. A BPM Roadmap provides the guidance to successfully adopt a business process oriented approach to building IT solutions to support the business. A well constructed BPM Roadmap defines projects that incrementally deliver BPM and provide business value at each step while minimizing the risks associated with BPM adoption. To build such a roadmap requires a solid understanding of the current situation with respect to BPM, a clear vision of what the BPM initiative needs to accomplish to be successful, and a structured approach to defining the iterations that comprise the path to successful BPM adoption.

  • Enterprise Security - This guide provides access to the material required for using OUM on Enterprise Security projects. The supplemental guidance is provided to assist in tailoring project plans, based on the security domains defined by the International Information Systems Security Certification Consortium (ISC)2™. You may also use this view to understand the work done by security architects during Enterprise Security implementation projects.

  • Oracle Support Services - The materials presented in this supplemental guide provide specific information for OUM practitioners to provide the guidance needed to effectively manage and support the lifecycle of Oracle environments during an implementation and after go-live.

  • Siebel Customer Relationship Management (CRM) - This guide supports the implementation of Oracle's Siebel Customer Relationship Management (CRM) products from sales, marketing and e-commerce, to customer service, multi-channel interaction and analytics.

  • Service-Oriented Architecture (SOA) Project Delivery - This guide is used to execute projects following the SOA approach, either as an initial project, or as subsequent projects. In this view, the focus is on delivery but it also provides the possibility to first plan the customer’s SOA strategy at the enterprise level.

  • Service-Oriented Architecture (SOA) Application Integration Architecture (AIA) - This guide is used to execute SOA implementations that utilize AIA. Oracle Application Integration Architecture provides an open standards based framework for creating cross-application business processes that support the way you run your business today, while paving the way for your long term, strategic, business transformation plans. Its application independent framework enables you to utilize the applications of your choice to create composite business processes unique to your business, on a flexible service-oriented architecture.

  • Service-Oriented Architecture (SOA) Governance Implementation - This guide provides detail information on processes and tasks that an organization can follow to implement the SOA Governance Model.

  • Service-Oriented Architecture (SOA) Engineering Planning - This guide is used to help organizations adjust or complement their project delivery methodology to support SOA execution. Many enterprises struggle with SOA adoption. One area of particular concern is the delivery of projects within their SOA. Many enterprises attempt to apply traditional software engineering methodologies (such as Unified Process, agile, waterfall, etc) that have not been adopted to handle the wider, cross-project reuse that must be addressed for projects participating in a SOA strategy. The SOA Engineering Planning view supports a service where the customer is taken through a series of facilitated workshops aimed at developing their own enterprise methodology for SOA projects.

  • Service-Oriented Architecture (SOA) Modeling Planning - This guide is used for the planning of modeling activities in conjunction with SOA project delivery, and the development of a Motivational Model (as part of the Business Strategy Identification), process and data models, and service meta models. A service based on this view can be executed in a series of four optional areas. This view is designed to prepare organizations to incorporate modeling into their service engineering process. It should be used as a guideline for engagements that are delivered to organizations that want to plan for modeling in their SOA delivery process and define models in support of such efforts.

  • Service-Oriented Architecture (SOA) Governance Planning - This guide is used to help organizations build a SOA Governance Model that manages the SOA key assets. Many enterprises struggle with SOA adoption, and one of the reasons is that the benefits of SOA do not come automatically. A company needs an appropriate SOA Governance Model that manages the SOA key assets such as Services, Reference Architecture and the Service Infrastructure Layer. Performing a planning engagement based on this view provides a roadmap for the adoption of SOA Governance tailored to the manner in which a given organization works. During the engagement, you work with the customer to capture relevant information about Governance structures and practices, facilitate the development of the Governance vision and work with the customer’s staff to develop the Governance Roadmap.

  • Service-Oriented Architecture (SOA) Reference Architecture Planning - This guide is used to help organizations develop an architectural vision for SOA, and a roadmap to execute against that vision. The vision defines a set of principles and architecture to create consistency across the enterprise. The roadmap defines a set of incremental steps to progress a customer from their current state of SOA infrastructure to their architectural vision.

  • Service-Oriented Architecture (SOA) Roadmap - This guide is based on the SOA Roadmap described in the IT Strategies from Oracle (ITSO) guidelines and reference architectures library, Creating an SOA Roadmap. A Service-Oriented Architecture (SOA) Roadmap provides the guidance to successfully adopt a service-oriented approach to building IT solutions to support the business. A well constructed SOA Roadmap defines projects that incrementally deliver SOA and provide business value at each step while minimizing the risks associated with SOA adoption. To build such a roadmap requires a solid understanding of the current situation with respect to SOA, a clear vision of what the SOA initiative needs to accomplish to be successful, and a structured approach to defining the iterations that comprise the path to successful SOA adoption.

  • WebCenter - This guide supports WebCenter project implementations. Oracle WebCenter is the user engagement platform for social businesses, connecting people and information. It brings together a portfolio of portal, web experience management, content, social and collaboration technologies into a single product suite.
