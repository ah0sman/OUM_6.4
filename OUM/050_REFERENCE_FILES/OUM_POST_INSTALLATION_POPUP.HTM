OUM

Oracle’s Full Lifecycle Method for Deploying Oracle-Based Business Solutions

The Oracle® Unified Method (OUM) supports implementations of every size including: projects (small to large), programs, agile implementations and staff augmentation engagements.

OUM includes support for a variety of cloud projects, from guiding customers in planning their transition to cloud, to SaaS implementations, to managed cloud services.

Go to the OUM Cloud Home Page.

To be most effective, OUM should be scaled to fit your implementation. You should do no more than is necessary to satisfy the requirements of the implementation and appropriately address risks.

The recommended approach for scaling OUM is to –

  • Review the OUM Implement Core Workflow View.
  • Develop your workplan from a core set of OUM tasks.
    • Consider the OUM Implement Core Workflow View or refer to the Tailoring OUM for Your Project guidance.
  • Add tasks to your workplan as you identify scope and risk.
    • For example, Business Rules, Services, Performance Management, Technical Architecture, Documentation, etc.
  • Consider the depth to which your project team will execute specific tasks during specific iterations.
    • Tasks are placeholders for work. They are highly scalable.
    • Tasks can vary from “a bit of thinking” to “development of a comprehensive work product".
    • It is often better to consider tasks than to completely eliminate tasks from your workplan.
  • Consider whether it is advisable to combine tasks or work products, or execute at the activity level.

Using this approach, you should be able to arrive at a balanced set of tasks and work products for your implementation. And remember - "Don't serve the method. Make it serve you".
