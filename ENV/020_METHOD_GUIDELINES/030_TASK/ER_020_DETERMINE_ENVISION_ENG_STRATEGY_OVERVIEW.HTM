ER.020 - DETERMINE ENVISION ENGAGEMENT STRATEGY

The purpose of this task is to validate that a particular engagement or approach you have in mind will be a good approach for the enterprise or you actually determine which approach will be the most suitable. Use this information to determine the strategy for the Envision engagement. This should include, in detail, a description of the kind of engagement and how this program fits into other existing programs. This could be anything from a smaller engagement to explore the use of a specific technology, such as a a Proof of Concept, or a larger Envision engagement to determine the strategies of the enterprise around several business areas and technologies that will require a full-blown Envision engagement to determine the strategies of the entire enterprise. Determine which method processes and which tasks in each process should be included, and what work products should be produced. You should also determine the scope of the area that should be covered, for example, should it cover the complete enterprise, or just a few departments.

DETAILED DESCRIPTION

In Envision we talk about performing an engagement using a specific approach.

  • The term, roadmap may reflect a high-level approach to achieve a defined goal. It may also contain a plan of projects or engagements that are needed to achieve that goal.
  • An engagement is an agreed upon set of activities that should be performed. This could be activities predefined in a plan, or it could be any given set of activities that you agree to perform, independent of any plan or program.
  • The approach explains how you want to perform the engagement. If you use an existing plan as a basis, often an approach is already suggested, but sometimes multiple approaches are suggested from which you could choose. If you do not use an existing plan, you should determine how you want to perform the engagement. Decisions you need to make may for example be when (for which tasks) and how many workshops you want to use, whether you plan to use multiple iterations, and so on.

During this task, you determine the approach on a high-level, while you produce the detailed plan as part of the Set and Agree on Plan for Envision Engagement task (ER.030).

APPROACH

Review Existing Plan(s) and Other Relevant Enterprise-Specific Material

Review existing plans and programs that may be affected by or that may impact the engagement that is being considered. Also review any other type of enterprise-level information that seems to be relevant to evaluate the suitability of the engagement, and the Engagement Strategy. It is important to obtain an initial understanding of the enterprise and the enterprise situation. The type of information to look for is typically:

  • Enterprise Business Overview
  • Enterprise IT Overview
  • Enterprise Influence Map
  • Applications/Technology Footprint
  • Known Business/IT Drivers
  • Strategic Plan

Review Current Relationship Between the Enterprise and Suppliers/Delivery Organizations

Perform a review of the current relationship between the enterprise and suppliers or delivery organizations that are of interest for the engagement. This is done to better understand what kind of engagement may be the most appropriate. Is there already an established relationship, and if so, on what level, and is it a positive relationship? Do we want to improve or change the relationship?

Determine Enterprise Characteristics

Certain characteristics of an enterprise make them more or less suitable for a specific engagement. If you are considering a specific approach, review any available guidelines to validate whether the engagement characteristics fit the enterprise characteristics.

Assess Suitability

Depending upon how well the enterprise characteristics fit are a fit for the characteristics for a certain type of engagement, make a decision on what kind of engagement or approach is the most appropriate.

Determine Suitable Approach

Determine the approach to take for the engagement. Review the method processes and determine which processes should be covered as part of the engagement. Review the customer situation to determine which activities, tasks and work products you will include.

Envision can be performed in multiple iterations or increments, for example, in one iteration you can focus on a specific process or business area, and you can expand or add detail in following iterations. Consider whether it is appropriate to suggest an iterative or incremental approach. Often, it is less risky to cover smaller areas first, and expand when you know the customer situation better, or when the customer has become more used to the process.

Obtain Approval for Chosen Approach

You should obtain approval from all required decision makers to continue with the chosen approach.

WORK PRODUCT DETAILS

Audience, Distribution and Usage

The Envision Engagement Strategy is used in the following ways:

  • to provide background on the enterprise characteristics that help set an initial direction for the engagement
  • to documents the characteristics and business drivers that are triggering the engagement, potentially between the enterprise and a delivery organization (such as, Oracle).
  • to provide all involved parties with a documented approach for the Envision engagement

Distribute the Envision Engagement Strategy as follows:

  • to key stakeholders from senior management for both the IT and the business areas of the enterprise
  • if a delivery organization is involved, also to key stakeholders from the delivery organization, as appropriate
