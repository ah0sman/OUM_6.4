[ISM] INFORMATION SECURITY MANAGEMENT

"Information security management (ISM) is a governance activity within the corporate governance framework. It provides the strategic direction and is the focal point for all security activities. It ensures the objectives are achieved, that information security risks are managed and that enterprise information resources are used responsibly."1

From an ITIL perspective, "the purpose of the ISM process is to align IT security with business security and to ensure it matches the agreed needs of the business"1.

OBJECTIVES

From an ITIL perspective,"the objective of information security management (ISM) is to protect the interests of those relying on information, and the systems and communications that deliver the information, from harm as a result of failures of confidentiality, integrity and availability".1

Back to Top

APPROACH

The Information Security Management process ensures that existing IT service management practices and processes support the information security requirements for the stakeholder business entities, including both Oracle and customers.

Details about existing core Oracle security practices are documented in the Security Practices schedule found on My Oracle Support (MOS). Per the Security Practices schedule: "Oracle security policies cover the management of security for both Oracle’s internal operations as well as the services Oracle provides to its customers. The policies apply to all Oracle employees. In some cases, Managed Cloud Services-specific standards and policies may exceed requirements mandated by the Oracle security policies, thus enforcing further controls within Managed Cloud Services operations and systems. These policies, which are aligned with the ISO/IEC 27002:2005 (formerly known as ISO/IEC 17799:2005) and ISO/IEC 27001:2005 standards, govern all areas of security applicable to Managed Cloud Services. The policies are confidential information and are not available for review by Customer or third parties."

It is strongly recommended that all customers review the Security Practices schedule to determine if Oracle's security practices meet existing requirements. Additional security services are available - customers should contact either their Oracle sales person or their SDM or TM.

Exceptions to standard security services/processes are reviewed and approved through the Managed Cloud Compliant Tracker System (MCCTS), which is an internal Oracle portal through which Oracle personnel interact. Review and approval time for non-standard security requests are typically 5 business days, but may be longer depending on the nature of the request.
