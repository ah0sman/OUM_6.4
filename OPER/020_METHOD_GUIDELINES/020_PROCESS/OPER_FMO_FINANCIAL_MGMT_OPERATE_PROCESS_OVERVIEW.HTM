[FMO] FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT - OPERATE

From an ITIL perspective, "[t]he purpose of financial management for IT services can be summarized as1:

  • To secure the appropriate level of funding to design, develop and deliver the IT services required to support the service strategy.
  • To ensure that the IT service provider does not commit to services that it is unable to deliver.
  • To identify the balance between service cost and quality, and supply and demand."

OBJECTIVES

From an ITIL perspective, objectives of the Financial Management process include1:

  • Defining and maintaining a framework to:secure funding to manage the provision of services; identify, manage and communicate the cost of providing services; recover costs of service provision
  • Accounting for the money spent in the development, delivery and support of services

APPROACH

Similar to the Service Portfolio Management process, for IT services delivered to Oracle customers, financial management processes are primarily Oracle internal matters. However, contracting and invoicing activities are the two primary areas where Oracle and customers should collaborate to share responsibilities for financial management for IT services.

Oracle contracts typically provide sufficient detail, based on the services purchased from the Oracle service catalog, for customers to understand the amount they are obligated to pay for each service included in the contract or amendment, and overall for the entire contract or amendment. Contracts may include price holds for specified periods of time over the contract term for some or all of the purchased services, and may also include pre-negotiated additional resource charges (ARC) and reduced resource credits (RRC) based on service usage over the contract term. With regard to invoicing, resources assigned to the customer's account, typically either the Transition Manager (TM) or Service Delivery Manager (SDM), play the primary role assisting customers to ensure that the Oracle invoicing process addresses customer requirements.

It is also highly recommended that customers closely monitor contractual entitlements as a means to avoid potential additional charges in the event that actual usage exceeds contracted entitlement levels. Many of Oracle services (see also Service Catalog Management) include entitlements for various service components, including labor, infrastructure, and operational events - customer contracts and associated service schedules available either in contracts or on My Oracle Support detail what these entitlements are. Oracle status reporting and/or governance deliverables typically include tracking and reporting of actuals to contracted entitlements.
