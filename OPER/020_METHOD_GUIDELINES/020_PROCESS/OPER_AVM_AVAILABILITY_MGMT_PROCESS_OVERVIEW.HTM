[AVM] AVAILABILITY MANAGEMENT

From an ITIL perspective, the purpose of the Availability Management process is to "ensure that the level of availability delivered in all IT Services meets the agreed availability needs in the cost-effective and timely manner. It is concerned with meeting both the current and future availability needs of the business."1

Back to Top

OBJECTIVES

From an ITIL perspective, the objectives of the Availability Management process include1:

  • "Producing and maintaining the availability plan, reflection the current and future needs of the business, and to provide guidance to the business and IT on availability-related issues."
  • "Ensuring that availability achievements meet or exceed targets and, where they do not assist with the diagnosis and resolution of related incidents and problems."
  • Assessing all changes for their impact on the availability plan and proactively improve availability, where cost-justifiable to do so."

APPROACH

In the context of all Oracle services listed on the service catalog, the scope of availability management includes activities in support of both existing and forecasted customers:

  • Availability of workforce required to support services.
  • Design, provisioning, and support of IT infrastructure that is used to provide services.
  • Provisioning and support of desktop and laptop hardware provided to Oracle resources supporting services.

As part of this scope, customers should be aware that, as a proactive availability management measure, Oracle performs scheduled maintenance for shared (shared across multiple customers) data center infrastructure on a rolling basis throughout the year. Details (including the purpose of the maintenance and expected impact on service availability) for these scheduled maintenance activities are available from the Oracle Service Delivery Manager (SDM) or Transition Manager (TM).

In the context of Oracle service engagements, requirements for service availability are discussed and documented during the sales and solution phase, and based on metrics such as average and maximum concurrent users, but may consider other inputs such as the need for redundancy or high availability (see also Design Coordination). During implementation, the solution design (OSA) and any feedback from the Service Validation and Testing process comprise the core inputs to the Availability Plan. During production status, availability is monitored to proactively detect events that may trigger a major incident (see also Event Management).

Oracle and customers typically discuss and review the Availability Plan as part of the Service Review; both the Availability Plan and Service Review are governance deliverables that apply for certain Oracle contracts. The Availability Plan topic during the Service Review typically includes discussion of performance against either service targets or agreed availability levels (see also Service Level Management), details on major incidents that occurred during a previous period, and any changes implemented, recommended, and/or planned that address(ed) resolution and/or prevention of these major incidents. Discussion of any accepted and approved change recommendations (planned changes) may also occur during review and discussion of the Service Plan (see also Change Management, Release and Deployment Management, Change Evaluation) - another governance deliver that may apply for certain contracts. Oracle customers should review their contract or consult with their SDM and/or TM to determine what governance deliverables apply for their contract(s) and what the frequency is for the review and discussion of these deliverables (see also Business Relationship Management).

Proactive discussion of availability may also occur in the context of Periodic Maintenance Plans (PMP). The PMP applies for certain contracts, and includes patching for technology updates and implementation of system assessment recommendations by Oracle teams. Customers should review their contract or consult with their SDM or TM to determine the extent to which a PMP applies to their contract.
