[ICM] INCIDENT MANAGEMENT

From an ITIL perspective, “the purpose of the Incident Management process is to restore normal service operation as quickly as possible and minimize the adverse impact on business operations, thus ensuring that agreed levels of service quality are maintained. ‘Normal service operation’ is defined as an operational state where services and configuration items (CIs) are performing within their agreed service and operational levels”1.

OBJECTIVES

From an ITIL perspective, objectives of the Incident Management process include1:

  • “Ensuring that standard methods and procedures are used for efficient and prompt response, analysis, documentation, ongoing management and reporting of incidents”.
  • “Increasing visibility and communication of incidents to business and IT support staff”.
  • “Enhancing business perception of IT through use of a professional approach in quickly resolving and communicating incidents when they occur”.
  • “Aligning incident management activities and priorities with those of the business”.
  • “Maintaining user satisfaction with the quality of IT services”.

APPROACH

Incidents within Oracle are managed through My Oracle Support as service requests (SRs). SRs are either automatically generated via Enterprise Manager (EM) monitoring (see also Event Management) or created by customers via My Oracle Support (MOS) or via the 24x7 Service Desk. Incidents can also be monitored by accessing the Incidents tab in the Oracle Pulse app for the iPad or via the external Pulse URL.

If included in the contract, incidents may be managed via the customers IT service management tool.

For SRs created via MOS, it is imperative that customers properly document the issue, including correctly indicating whether the incident (issue) type is a Service Outage, Service Issue (Non Outage), or an Oracle Product Issue. Unless you are absolutely certain that an incident is a product issue and should be routed to Oracle Global Product Support (GPS), it is recommended that Oracle customers log all incidents as either a type of Service Outage or Service Issue (Non Outage). Triage is performed on all incidents routed to Oracle operations and as appropriate routed to GPS.

Unless a customer has purchased Functional Service Desk services, the customer plays a primary role in managing both operational incidents and product support incidents. Customers should monitor the status of incidents, including any change of status to Customer Working in the case where Oracle operations or GPS engineers need additional information to further investigation into the cause of the incident.

When experiencing dissatisfaction regarding progress with incident investigation, customers should start the formal escalation process. The escalation process increases the level of engagement of Oracle management with the incident investigation process. The formal escalation process includes (1) an update to the SR with (a) request to escalate, (b) expectations of escalation (i.e. raise severity, duty manager call back, etc.), (c) justification for the escalation, including impact of the SR to the business and/or implementation (include key milestone dates). Note that requests for 24x7 efforts require a customer contact, including cell phone details for Oracle personnel to reach specified customer contact(s) during off hours.

Should customer efforts to escalate not result in satisfactory progress, the next step is to contact the assigned service delivery manager (SDM) and/or TM.
