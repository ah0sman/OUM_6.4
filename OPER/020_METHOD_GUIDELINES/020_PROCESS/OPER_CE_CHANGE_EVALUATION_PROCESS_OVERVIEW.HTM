[CE] CHANGE EVALUATION

From an ITIL perspective, “the purpose of the Change Evaluation process is to provide a consistent and standardized means of determining the performance of a service change in the context of likely impacts on business outcomes, and on existing proposed services and IT infrastructure.”1

Back to Top

OBJECTIVES

From an ITIL perspective, the objectives of change evaluation include1:

  • "Setting stakeholder expectation correctly".
  • "Evaluating the intended effects of a service change and as much of the unintended effects as is reasonably practical".
  • "Providing good-quality outputs so that change management can expedite an effective decision about whether or not a service change is to be authorized".

Back to Top

APPROACH

Changes to Oracle services may occur either across multiple/all customers (i.e. scheduled maintenance for shared data center infrastructure) or for a single customer. For service changes that occur across multiple/all customers, Oracle distributes email notifications to provide details of changes, including expected impact to services such as planned downtime. Customers can address questions about these notifications through their SDM and/or TM.

For service changes that impact a single customer, these changes may be driven by either Oracle (i.e. technical/infrastructure changes) or the customer. As such, it is strongly recommended that customers coordinate and collaborate with their SDM and/or TM to leverage Oracle advice and guidance as appropriate to understand the nature of the change and expected impact, including any downtime. Customers are responsible for managing end user expectations, and for defining and executing an effective and standardized method for change evaluation (see also Service Validation and Testing).
