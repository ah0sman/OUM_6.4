[CPM] CAPACITY MANAGEMENT

From an ITIL perspective, “the purpose of capacity management is to ensure that the capacity of IT services and the IT infrastructure meets the agreed capacity and performance-related requirements in a cost-effective and timely manner. Capacity management is a process that extends across the whole service lifecycle. A key factor in managing capacity is ensuring it is considered in service design. The capacity management process provides a focal point for the management of all IT performance and capacity issues.”1

OBJECTIVES

From an ITIL perspective, “the objectives of Capacity Management include"1:

  • "Producing and maintaining an accurate capacity plan, and providing advice and guidance on all capacity and performance-related issues".
  • "Ensuring service performance achievements meet their agreed targets, and assisting with diagnosis and resolution of incidents and problems.
  • "Assessing the impact of all changes on the capacity plan and proactively improve performance, where cost-effective.”1

APPROACH

In the context of all Oracle services listed on the service catalog, the scope of capacity management includes the following planning activities in support of both existing and forecasted customers:

  • Planning and monitoring capacity in terms of human, technical, information and financial resources requirements.
  • Planning capacity for existing, enhanced, and new service offering requirements.

In the context of Oracle service engagements, requirements for service capacity are discussed and documented during the sales and solution phase, and based on metrics such as average and maximum concurrent users, but may consider other inputs such as current, changed, or planned vital business functions (see also Design Coordination).

During implementation, the solution design (OSA) and any feedback from the Service Validation and Testing process comprise the core inputs to a capacity plan. During production status, capacity is monitored using metrics such as CPU and memory usage (see also Event Management). Oracle and customers typically discuss and review performance trends as part of the Service Review, which is a governance deliverable that applies for certain Oracle contracts. The capacity plan topic during the Service Review typically includes discussion of performance during a specified period, any changes implemented to improve performance, and any recommended, and/or planned changes. Discussion of any accepted and approved change recommendations (planned changes) may also occur during review and discussion of the Service Plan (see also Change Management, Release and Deployment Management, Change Evaluation) - another governance deliver that may apply for certain contracts. Oracle customers should review their contract or consult with their SDM or TM to determine what governance deliverables apply for their contract(s) and what the frequency is for the review and discussion of these deliverables (see also Business Relationship Management).

Proactive discussion of capacity may also occur in the context of Periodic Maintenance Plans (PMP). The PMP applies for certain contracts, and includes patching for technology updates and implementation of system assessment recommendations by Oracle teams. Customers should review their contract or consult with their SDM or TM to determine the extent to which a PMP applies to their contract.
