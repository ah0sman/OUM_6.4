[SVT] SERVICE VALIDATION AND TESTING

From an ITIL perspective, "the purpose of the Service Validation and Testing process is to ensure that a new or changed IT service matches its design specification and meets the needs of the business"1.

Back to Top

OBJECTIVES

From an ITIL perspective, the objectives of the Service Validation and Testing process include1:

  • "Providing confidence that a release will create a new or changed service that delivers the expected outcomes and value for the customers within the projected costs, capacity, and constraints".
  • "Validating that a service is fit for purpose - it will deliver the required utility".
  • "Providing assurance that a service is fit for use - it will deliver the agreed warranty".
  • "Confirming that customer and stakeholder requirements are correctly defined".
  • "Identifying, assessing, and addressing issues, errors, and risks throughout service transition".

Back to Top

APPROACH

For new services that are introduced to the Oracle service catalog, service validation and testing is primarily an Oracle internal matter. For changes to existing Oracle services, requirements from Oracle customers and the Oracle continual service improvement process (see also Continual Service Improvement) serve as key inputs.

In the context of Oracle service engagements, Oracle delivery teams collaborate closely with customers to ensure new and changed services move efficiently and effectively into production and operational use. The SDM and/or the TM are typically the primary contact points for customers to coordinate the service transition resources and activities, including helping customers successfully execute service validation and testing activities.

For Oracle customers, during the Service Transition phase for all new or changed services, customers are responsible for validating that purchased Oracle services address their business requirements. Review and acceptance of the solution and the Service Initialization Report (SIR) by the customer marks a key milestone for provisioning of infrastructure and/or software to begin. In some cases, multiple reviews/revision of the solution and SIR may be necessary to fully provision all infrastructure and applications.

Customers with implementations of new services and/or major service upgrades are required to participate in the Oracle Production Assessment (PA) process, which primarily ensures that customers' systems and environments are compliant with Oracle configuration and supportability standards. What is required by PA varies by service, but all significant new technology implementations and/or upgrades should include formal customer acceptance prior to any transition of the service into the production or Service Operation phase. Formal customer acceptance should include sign off, as a result of functional testing, and no open showstopper incidents should exist for all critical business processes. Additionally, because the Oracle solution design typically only incorporates a limited number of sizing inputs (including modules to be implemented, and average/maximum concurrent users), Oracle strongly recommends that all customers implement automated performance/load testing to ensure that customer use cases are adequately tested for performance. This is particularly important for custom-developed or 3rd party solutions. Depending on the complexity of their application use cases, customers may also want to consider automated quality testing. Oracle offers both load and regression testing services as part of its Extended Services catalog

Parties to Oracle engagements should consider service validation for both technical-based services (hardware, software, infrastructure, etc.) and labor-based services. Typically, the Transition Planning and Support process addresses readiness for labor-based services - as such it is strongly recommended that all Oracle engagements include at least an informal assessment of process readiness in terms of customers' process integration with Oracle processes. Given the level of process integration that is required between Oracle and its customers, validation and testing of process readiness is also highly critical. For example, for redundancy purposes, multiple customer contacts should have access to My Oracle Support (MOS) to be able to create and manage incidents (service requests or SRs) and requests for change (RFCs) and be familiar with how to effectively manage incidents and RFCs through MOS and the Oracle Service Desk (24x7 phone access). See also Transition Planning and Support.
