[DC] DESIGN COORDINATION

From an ITIL perspective, "the purpose of [the] Design Coordination [process] is to ensure the goals and objectives of the design [phase] are met. It provides a single point of coordination and control for all activities and processes within this [phase] of the service lifecycle. "1

Back to Top

OBJECTIVES

From an ITIL perspective, objectives of the Design Coordination process include1:

  • "Ensuring the consistent design of appropriate services, service management information systems, architecture, technology, process, information and metrics to meet current and evolving business needs and requirements."
  • "Planning and coordinating all design activities."
  • "Producing Service Design Plans (SDP) based on service charters and change requests."
  • "Ensuring that appropriate service designs are produced and that they are handed over to the service transition as agreed."
  • "Managing the interfaces with service strategy and service transition."
  • "Improving the efficiency and effectiveness of service design activities and processes."

Back to Top

APPROACH

For the design of Oracle services listed on the service catalog, the Design Coordination process is primarily an Oracle internal matter.

However, Oracle services as contracted with customers may require a solution design with custom components, including infrastructure (compute, storage, network), platform (software), labor, or operational events (backups, refreshes, etc.). To facilitate the Design Coordination process, Oracle typically assigns a Solution Manager to walk through a discovery process where requirements and available services are discussed. The Solution Manager prepares a design proposal, which is then finalized and contracted based on customer review and acceptance. Upon receipt of a fully executed contract and prior to the provisioning of any services, the delivery team, which typically includes the Service Delivery Manager (SDM) and/or Transition Manager (TM), engages with the Solution Manager to review and validate with the customer the Service Initialization Report (SIR), which documents solution requirements. Changes to the solution design identified at this point may require a contract amendment and the Oracle sales team engages as necessary. Once customers review and accept the solution, Oracle technical architects then prepare the final solution architecture (or OSA), which is then used as the basis for provisioning. Solution design changes for some services, including labor and certain infrastructure and operational event changes, may not require the solution review and acceptance steps as indicated here, but may require a contract amendment. Regardless of whether services are in implementation or in production, the solution design process is the same.
