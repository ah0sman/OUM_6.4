[PM] PROBLEM MANAGEMENT

From an ITIL perspective, “the purpose of the Problem Management process is to manage problems through their lifecycle from first identification through investigation, documentation and eventual resolution and closure. Problem management seeks to minimize the adverse impact of incidents and problems on the business caused by underlying errors within the IT infrastructure, and to proactively prevent recurrence of incidents related to these errors”1.

OBJECTIVES

From an ITIL perspective, objectives of the Problem Management process include1:

  • Preventing problems and resulting incidents from happening”.
  • Eliminating recurring incidents”.
  • Minimizing the impact of incidents that cannot be prevented”.

Back to Top

APPROACH

Oracle maintains a Problem Management team that works both proactively and reactively to prevent the occurrence and re-occurrence of major incidents (see also Incident Management). This team also works to minimize the business impact of major incidents.

Core activities of the Problem Management team include:

  • Maintaining the known error database (KEDB) where problem records are created for the team to investigate.
  • Performing root cause analyses (RCAs) in the context of major incidents that impact multiple customers or a single customer.
  • Performing process RCAs in the context of quality and service operation execution issues.
  • Recommending workarounds to minimize the impact of major incidents.
  • Analyzing operational data to identify triggering conditions or events that cause major incidents.
  • Recommending actions to improve operational quality, and to prevent major incident occurrence and re-occurrence.

For Oracle Product Support issues, the knowledge management function of My Oracle Support (MOS) also serves as a key capability in the problem management process.

When reviewing recommendations (CAs) and outputs from the problem management process, customers should strongly consider that any internal customer change advisory boards (CABs), including the emergency CAB, include Oracle resources. Also, customers may be taking significant risks when recommendations from the Oracle Problem Management team are not implemented on a timely basis (see also Change Management, Release and Deployment Management).
