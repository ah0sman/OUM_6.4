CONTINUAL SERVICE IMPROVEMENT

From an ITIL perspective, "the purpose of the Continual Service Improvement [phase] is to describe best practice for achieving incremental and large scale improvements in service quality, operational efficiency and business continuity, and for ensuring that the service portfolio continues to be aligned to business needs"1.

Back to Top 

OBJECTIVES

From an ITIL perspective, objectives for the Continual Service Improvement phase include1:

  • "Reviewing, analyzing, prioritizing, and making recommendations on improvement opportunities in each lifecycle stage: service strategy, service design, service transition, service operation, and CSI itself".
  • "Reviewing and analyze service level achievement"..
  • "Identifying and implementing specific activities to improve IT service quality and improve the efficiency and effectiveness of the enabling processes".
  • "Improving cost effectiveness of delivering IT services without sacrificing customer satisfaction".
  • "Ensuring applicable quality management methods are used to support continual improvement activities".
  • "Ensuring that processes have clearly defined objectives and measurements that lead to actionable improvements".
  • "Understanding what to measure, why it is being measured and what the successful outcome should be".

APPROACH

For Oracle customers, continual service improvement at the service catalog level is primarily an Oracle internal matter. However, appropriate channels, including the Oracle Customer Advisory Board (OCAB) exist for customers to channel their feedback. At the customer engagement level, various processes exist for continual service improvement (see also Business Relationship Management). For example, as part of the Service Review governance deliverable, service levels are reviewed for efficiency and efficacy, and improvements and remediation plans are discussed, implemented, and evaluated. The Periodic Maintenance Plan (PMP) represents another key aspect of continual service improvement. PMPs are part of most core Oracle offerings and are detailed as part of the Services Entitlements Schedules available on My Oracle Support. PMPs typically include implementation of system assessment recommendations. Oracle highly recommends that all customer engagements focus not just on technical systems improvements but also process improvements, particularly in the area of process integration between Oracle and the customer.
