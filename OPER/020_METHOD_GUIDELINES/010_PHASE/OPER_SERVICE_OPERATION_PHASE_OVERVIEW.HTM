SERVICE OPERATION

From an ITIL perspective, "the purpose of the Service Operation [phase] of the service lifecycle is to coordinate and carry out the activities and processes required to deliver and manage services at agreed levels to business users and customers. Service operation is also responsible for the ongoing management of the technology that is used to deliver and support services"1.

Back to Top 

OBJECTIVES

From an ITIL perspective, objectives for the Service Operation phase include1:

  • "Maintaining business satisfaction and confidence in IT through effective and efficient delivery and support of agreed IT services".
  • "Minimizing the impact of service outages on day-to-day business activities".
  • "Ensuring that access to agreed IT services is only provided to those authorized to receive those services".

APPROACH

For Oracle customers, once services are in production status, the primary interfaces to assist with management of purchased services vary depending on the contract, but may include My Oracle Support (MOS), the Service Desk, and the SDM and/or TM. An additional interface may be Oracle Pulse.

Agreed upon services, roles and responsibilities, and service levels (including all entitlements and any service level agreements that may apply) are governed by the contracted terms and conditions, including schedules that are either referenced in contracts and downloaded from My Oracle Support, or embedded in contracts executed by Oracle and customers. Effective and efficient service operation processes and procedures are a shared responsibility between Oracle and customers.
