BT.050 - REVIEW CONTRACT WITH CUSTOMER

The purpose of this task is for the project manager to conduct a review of the contract with the customer in order to gain a common understanding of the project and clarify any issues. It is essential to the overall success of the project for the project manager and the customer stakeholders to have a clear and common understanding of the scope, objectives, approach, assumptions and contractual terms governing the project.

APPROACH

Prepare for Meeting

In preparation for the meeting, prepare an agenda. Consider conducting one meeting to review the contract and the project approach (BT.060).

Review the Contract with the Customer

Conduct a contract review with the appropriate customer stakeholders (customer project sponsor, customer project manager, third-party project managers) to:

  • Facilitate a common understanding of the project.
  • Ensure that everyone has the same interpretation and expectations.
  • Establish a positive foundational relationship for the project.
  • Clarify any issues or concerns.

This meeting and review is not to make any agreements or commitments that deviate from what is defined in the contract. It is only to make sure all parties have the same interpretation.

Be particularly aware of customer claims regarding verbal agreements that are not part of the written contractual scope or contained in supporting documents. It is essential that you do not make any agreements or commitments during the reviews that deviate from what is defined in the contract. Be sure to take comprehensive minutes during the review.

Gain Common Understanding of Project Roles

Gain a common understanding of the project roles for both your organization and the customer organization in order to set appropriate expectations and make sure the project will be staffed appropriately.

Gain Common Understanding of Deliverables and Acceptance Criteria

Gain a common understanding of the project deliverables and acceptance criteria. Take advantage of this meeting to clarify any issues.

Document Any Action Items/Issues

Document any action items, issues or areas of disconnect or concern.  Follow up on these items in accordance with your organization’s policies.
