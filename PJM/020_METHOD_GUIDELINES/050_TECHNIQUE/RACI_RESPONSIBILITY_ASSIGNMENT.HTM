RACI-RESPONSIBILITY ASSIGNMENT

The goal of this technique is to prepare and use a RACI Matrix to document the participation and the level of responsibility of organizational roles to tasks, deliverables,activities and processes. RACI is an acronym for Responsible, Accountable, Consulted and Informed. This technique is especially useful in tasks where governance or responsibility of processes is being defined. Preparation of the RACI Matrix helps bring clarity to defined levels of responsibility.

TECHNIQUE RESULTS

RACI Matrix - The RACI Matrix documents the responsibilities for portions of a project. It is also referred to as a RACI Chart, a Responsibility Assignment Matrix (RAM) and even a ARCI Matrix.

TECHNIQUE STEPS

No. Step Component Description
1 Create a matrix. RACI Matrix The RACI Matrix can be a table in a Microsoft Word document or an Excel spreadsheet. The RACI Matrix should have an many rows as there are processes or activities and as many columns as there are participating organizations.
2 List processes. RACI Matrix - Processes The first column of the RACI Matrix contains a short name describing the process (business process or simple work activities) using a verb-noun phrase or sentence.
3 Fill out the organization names. RACI Matrix - Organization Names The first row of the RACI Matrix, at the top of each column contains the organization names.
4 Fill out the level of responsibility. RACI Matrix - Responsibility or Level of Participation Each intersection of Process and Organization of the RACI Matrix contains the level of responsibility as follows: R=Responsible, A=Accountable, C=Consulted, I=Informed.
5 Verify content. RACI Matrix Verify the level of responsibility with each organization.

APPROACH

The RACI Matrix is a type of linear responsibility chart (LRC). The matrix describes the participation by various roles in completing tasks (may also be used for deliverables) for a project or business process.

During Requirements Gathering, a RACI Matrix is a simple way to document the level of responsibility or participation of individual organizations in each business process or project activity under discussion. At this time, it is a helpful tool to document the level of participation of each organization. A RACI Matrix may be used to capture the business processes and their associated participants during guided discussions in Requirements Gathering workshops.

It is a good idea to keep the individual processes stated simply as a verb-noun phrase similar to structure used for use cases. Include a Key or Legend that lists the full names for any abbreviated names within the organizational structure. You should also include the RACI Key for users who are new to using this technique.

A RACI Matrix is helpful in defining governance: who is the main owner of a process and who are the participants and owners of the business process. It can often uncover a mismatch in actual responsibilities and the perceptions of who is actually making the ownership decisions on what will happen in a process.

Later in the project, the RACI Matrix can be used to assist in many activities such as Business Process Improvement Reviews, User Acceptance Testing, or simply as a means of documenting the organizations and the work that they do in order to participate and accomplish the activities required to support the business.
